Do Businesses Really Need Tech?

Unit 1: Outcome 1 - 1.1 Describe the role of IT in modern business.

According to the Cambridge Dictionary, Information Technology (IT) is....

"The science and activity of using computers and other electronic equipment to store and send information."

Sticking to this definition, businesses have been using electronic equipment since the 19th century. Since 1975, when the first computers were invented, their uptake accelerated.

However, it wasn't until the mid-90s that computers, twinned with the internet, shifted from 'nice to have' to 'must have' in offices around the globe.

Over the last 25 years, technology has moved at an incredible pace and changed our lives indescribably.

In terms of businesses, the shift from analogue communication, data and image recording methods to digital models has perhaps been the most striking.

To demonstrate the magnitude of this change and just how much businesses rely on IT today, we can compare the operations of a medium-sized business today to one operating in the mid-to-late 1990s.

Businesses in The 1990s

  1. Used print channels such as brochures and flyers to market products and services.

  2. Used analogue film cameras and video cameras with tapes to capture images and moving pictures.

  3. Relied on telephones for internal and external communication.

  4. Managed customer calls through a switchboard system.

  5. Accessed the internet occasionally via cables over telephone networks.

  6. Accessed the internet occasionally via cables over telephone networks.

  7. Spent time and resource scanning and sending documents via.fax.

  8. Used resource, time and expenses for face-to-face meetings.

  9. Employed people to sort through and send post daily.

  10. Had secretaries taking meeting minutes manually in shorthand.

  11. Captured customer data on paper or in spreadsheets

  12. llocated space to storing lockable filing cabinets full of data and records.

  13. Tracked, measured and reported ROI with spreadsheets and face-to-face presentations.

  14. Communicated with stakeholders through physical AGMs and annual reports.

  15. Issued press announcements via. news agencies and news wire systems.

Businesses in 2020

Fast forward to the year 2020. The same processes may look something like the below.

  1. Markets their products through digital channels, such as email marketing, PPC, affiliate marketing and social media.

  2. Uses digital cameras and mobile phones to capture images and videos.

  3. Offers customers multiple communication channels, including email, phone, live chat, Q&As, and social media.

  4. Holds meetings remotely across multiple timezones with any number of participants using presentation software such as Powerpoint or Prezi and comms software such as Skype, Microsoft Teams or Zoom.

  5. Accesses the internet 24/7 through the wireless fidelity system (WIFI), with no need for a physical wired network between sender and receiver.

  6. In place of secretaries, employees manage and organise their own notes, comms, expenses, travel bookings and annual leave instantly 24/7 with software such as Microsoft One Note, Prezi, Trello, Workday and Concur.

  7. Captures customer data using software such as Salesforce and Sage.

  8. Tracks and reports metrics and ROI using software such as Salesforce, Talkwalker, Hootsuite and SurveyMonkey.

  9. Issues announcements to the press via. their websites and distributes news to the press using email, social media or software such as Cision Point.

  10. Stores customer and employee data securely using onsite, local or cloud servers.

As this comparison illustrates, from HR to finance, from marketing to sales, from managing sensitive data to booking a meeting room, IT is vital to the running of any business in 2020.
